Public Records Act Request

Request access to Downtown Manteca District public records in accordance with California Government Code Section 6250 et seq.

California Public Records Act

The California Public Records Act (PRA) provides the public with the right to access information concerning the conduct of the people's business. The Downtown Manteca District is committed to transparency and provides access to public records as required by law.

📋 What Are Public Records?

Public records include any writing containing information relating to the conduct of the public's business prepared, owned, used, or retained by the Downtown Manteca District, regardless of physical form or characteristics.

Examples include: Meeting minutes, agendas, contracts, correspondence, financial records, reports, policies, and procedures.

Request Process

You can submit a public records request using the form below, or by contacting us directly. We will respond to your request within 10 days as required by law.

Response Timeframe

  • Initial Response: Within 10 days of receiving your request
  • Determination: We will inform you whether records exist and when they will be available
  • Production: Records will be produced as soon as reasonably possible
  • Complex Requests: May require additional time; we will provide an estimated completion date

⚠️ Exempt Records

Some records may be exempt from disclosure under the Public Records Act, including:

  • Personnel records and files
  • Records subject to attorney-client privilege
  • Records that would compromise security
  • Preliminary drafts and notes
  • Records exempted by other statutes
