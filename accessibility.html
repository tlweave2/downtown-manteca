Web Accessibility Statement

Downtown Manteca District is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people with disabilities.

Our Commitment to Accessibility

The Downtown Manteca District is committed to ensuring that our website is accessible to all users, including those with disabilities. We strive to provide an inclusive digital experience that allows everyone to access information about our services, programs, and activities.

We are actively working to enhance the accessibility and usability of our website and ensure compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA standards.

Accessibility Standards

Our website aims to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA. These guidelines explain how to make web content accessible to people with a wide range of disabilities including:

  • Visual impairments (including blindness, low vision, and color blindness)
  • Hearing impairments (including deafness and hearing loss)
  • Motor impairments (including difficulty using a mouse or keyboard)
  • Cognitive disabilities (including learning disabilities and difficulties with concentration)

Accessibility Features

Our website includes the following accessibility features:

  • Alternative text for images and graphics
  • Keyboard navigation support
  • Proper heading structure for screen readers
  • High contrast color schemes
  • Descriptive link text
  • Resizable text without loss of functionality
  • Form labels and error identification
  • Skip navigation links

Ongoing Improvements

We continuously work to improve the accessibility of our website. Our ongoing efforts include:

  • Regular accessibility audits and testing
  • Staff training on accessibility best practices
  • User testing with people with disabilities
  • Consultation with accessibility experts
  • Updates to content and design based on feedback

Third-Party Content

While we strive to ensure accessibility across our entire website, some content may be provided by third parties. We work with our vendors and partners to ensure their content meets accessibility standards, but we cannot guarantee the accessibility of all external content.

Assistive Technology Compatibility

Our website has been designed and tested to work with commonly used assistive technologies, including:

  • Screen readers (NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver)
  • Voice recognition software
  • Keyboard navigation
  • Screen magnification software

Alternative Formats

We can provide information in alternative formats upon request, including:

  • Large print documents
  • Audio recordings
  • Braille materials
  • Electronic formats compatible with screen readers
  • Sign language interpretation for meetings

Feedback Process

When you contact us about an accessibility issue, we will:

  • Acknowledge receipt of your feedback within 2 business days
  • Investigate the issue promptly
  • Provide a substantive response within 10 business days
  • Work with you to resolve the accessibility barrier
  • Document the issue to prevent future occurrences

Compliance Monitoring

This accessibility statement was last reviewed and updated on January 2025. We review and update this statement regularly to reflect our ongoing accessibility improvements and compliance efforts.

The Downtown Manteca District is subject to the ADA Title II requirements for web accessibility. We are committed to meeting the WCAG 2.1 Level AA compliance deadline of April 24, 2027, as applicable to our organization.
