Privacy Policy

Learn how the Downtown Manteca District collects, uses, and protects your personal information.

Last Updated: January 2025

Introduction

The Downtown Manteca District ("we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website or interact with our services.

As a public entity, we are committed to transparency while also respecting your privacy rights. This policy complies with applicable federal and state privacy laws, including the California Information Practices Act.

🔒 Our Commitment

We collect only the information necessary to provide our services effectively and operate transparently as a public entity. We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for commercial purposes.

Information We Collect

Information You Provide Directly

We collect information you voluntarily provide when you:

  • Submit forms on our website (contact forms, business directory applications, public records requests)
  • Subscribe to our newsletter
  • Attend public meetings or events
  • Contact us via email or phone
  • Participate in public comment periods

Types of Personal Information Collected:

Information Type Examples Purpose
Contact Information Name, email address, phone number, mailing address Communication, service delivery, public notices
Business Information Business name, description, hours, social media handles Business directory listings, economic development
Public Records Requests Request details, purpose, preferred format Fulfilling legal obligations under California PRA
Meeting Participation Name, affiliation, comments made Meeting minutes, public record compliance

Information Collected Automatically

When you visit our website, we automatically collect certain technical information:

  • IP address
  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system
  • Pages visited and time spent
  • Referring website
  • Device information

Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Remember your preferences
  • Analyze website usage
  • Improve functionality
  • Provide relevant content

⚠️ Third-Party Services

Our website integrates with third-party services including Google Sheets, Google Calendar, and Google Maps. These services may collect information according to their own privacy policies. We recommend reviewing their privacy policies for details on how your information is handled.

How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for legitimate governmental purposes, including:

Service Delivery

  • Processing public records requests
  • Maintaining business directory listings
  • Sending newsletters and updates
  • Responding to inquiries and comments
  • Organizing and conducting public meetings

Legal Compliance

  • Meeting Brown Act and Public Records Act requirements
  • Maintaining official records
  • Complying with audit and oversight requirements
  • Responding to legal requests

Communication

  • Sending important district notifications
  • Publishing meeting agendas and minutes
  • Sharing community events and updates
  • Emergency communications

Website Improvement

  • Analyzing usage patterns
  • Improving accessibility
  • Enhancing user experience
  • Troubleshooting technical issues

Information Sharing and Disclosure

Public Records

As a public entity, certain information we collect may be subject to disclosure under the California Public Records Act (Government Code Section 6250 et seq.). This may include:

  • Communications with the district
  • Meeting attendance records
  • Public comments and correspondence
  • Business directory information

When We Share Information

We may share your personal information in the following circumstances:

  • Public Records Requests: As required by law
  • Legal Requirements: When compelled by subpoena, court order, or legal process
  • Service Providers: With vendors who assist in website operation and maintenance
  • Partner Agencies: When necessary for collaborative projects or services
  • Emergency Situations: To protect health, safety, or legal rights

Information We Do NOT Share

  • We do not sell personal information to commercial entities
  • We do not rent mailing lists
  • We do not share information for marketing purposes
  • We do not disclose information exempt from public disclosure

Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal information:

Technical Safeguards

  • SSL encryption for data transmission
  • Secure server hosting
  • Regular security updates
  • Access controls and authentication
  • Data backup and recovery procedures

Administrative Safeguards

  • Limited access to personal information
  • Staff training on privacy practices
  • Regular privacy policy reviews
  • Incident response procedures

⚠️ Security Limitation

While we implement reasonable security measures, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security of your information.

Your Rights and Choices

Access and Correction

You have the right to:

  • Request access to personal information we maintain about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Request updates to your business directory listing
  • Unsubscribe from our newsletter

Communication Preferences

You can manage your communication preferences by:

  • Using the unsubscribe link in our emails
  • Contacting us directly to update preferences
  • Opting out of non-essential communications

Cookie Management

You can control cookies through your browser settings:

  • Block or delete cookies
  • Set preferences for cookie acceptance
  • Receive notifications when cookies are set

📋 Important Note

Some information may be retained as required by law or necessary for legitimate governmental purposes, even after you request removal or correction.

Data Retention

We retain personal information only as long as necessary for the purposes for which it was collected or as required by law:

Retention Periods

Information Type Retention Period Legal Basis
Meeting records and public comments Permanent Public Records Act, Brown Act
Public records requests 3 years Government Code requirements
Business directory information Until removal requested or business closure Operational necessity
Newsletter subscriptions Until unsubscribed Consent-based
Website analytics data 26 months Google Analytics default

Children's Privacy

Our website is not directed to children under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we learn that we have collected personal information from a child under 13, we will take steps to delete such information promptly.

If you believe we have collected information from a child under 13, please contact us immediately.

Third-Party Websites

Our website may contain links to third-party websites, including:

  • Partner organization websites
  • Social media platforms
  • Business websites in our directory
  • Government agency websites

This Privacy Policy does not apply to third-party websites. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party sites you visit.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or applicable law. When we make changes, we will:

  • Update the "Last Updated" date
  • Post the revised policy on our website
  • Notify users of significant changes via email or website notice
  • Maintain previous versions for reference

Your continued use of our website after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the updated Privacy Policy.
